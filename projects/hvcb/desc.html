High Voltage Power Distribution Unit

This is a High Voltage Power Distribution Unit (HVPDU) designed and built while interning at Navier, an electric hydrofoiling maritime startup. The unit streamlines the hardware from two previously installed power distribution systems into a single, efficient unit.

The HVPDU serves as the primary high voltage control for the boat's dual rear motors, directing and regulating power flow from the battery supply. This consolidation reduced space consumption within the boat's electronics bay by approximately 1 cubic meter and cut 40kg of weight.

The project involved both mechanical enclosure design and electrical layout of fuses, contactors, and high-voltage connectors. The system was modeled in Solidworks, manufactured in China, and assembled on-site in Alameda. This project provided invaluable experience in product realization, including parts sourcing and manufacturer liaison work.
